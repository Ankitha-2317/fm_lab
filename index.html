Experiment 2: Bank Deposit Offer Decision

A bank offers an investor to deposit ₹16,000 and receive ₹1,800 annually forever.

Given Information:

  • Initial Investment: ₹16,000
  • Annual Cash Flow: ₹1,800

Case 1: Rate of Return = 12%

  • Present Value of Perpetuity: ₹15,000
  • Decision: Offer will be rejected as the present value is less than the initial investment.

Case 2: Rate of Return = 10%

  • Present Value of Perpetuity: ₹18,000
  • Decision: Offer will be accepted as the present value is greater than the initial investment.

Experiment 6: Internal Rate of Return (IRR) Calculation

A firm is evaluating a project costing ₹160,000 and generating future cash flows.

Given Information:

  • Initial Investment: ₹160,000
  • Hurdle Rate: 12%
  • Cash Flows Over 5 Years:
    • Year 1: ₹40,000
    • Year 2: ₹60,000
    • Year 3: ₹50,000
    • Year 4: ₹50,000
    • Year 5: ₹40,000

IRR Calculation Using PV Factors:

Year Cash Flow (₹) PV Factor @ 15% Present Value (₹) @ 15% PV Factor @ 16% Present Value (₹) @ 16%

Total PV:₹161,540₹157,750

IRR Calculation:

Using formula:
IRR = Lower Rate + [(PV @ Lower Rate - Initial Cost) / (PV @ Lower Rate - PV @ Higher Rate)] × (Higher Rate - Lower Rate)

IRR = 15.40%

Conclusion:

Since **IRR (15.40%) > Hurdle Rate (12%)**, the project should be accepted.
